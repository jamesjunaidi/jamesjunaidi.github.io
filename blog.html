The Halfway Point of the Year

Updated July 6, 2020

So, we have finally reached July. That means we are done with half of 2020. This year has indeed been crazy. I never imagined I would have spent my entire spring quarter remotely, or my internship remotely as well. Professionally, I feel like I missed out on a lot of development and connections that I would've had if I was on campus, or even in the office for my internship. But realistically, the remote options have been better than nothing. Kudos to T-Mobile for putting together an actually great remote internship program. I feel that even though I'm missing out on meeting people, I'm also able to connect with nationwide interns that I probably would've otherwise not had the chance too. Most of the interns seem to be in Washington, and I've been able to connect with quite a few of them and I don't know if I would've had that chance had the internship been in person. I think you can find a bright side to most situations that you may not find ideal, and that will help your mindset when bad things come along.

Finished with my first year at UCD!

Updated June 6, 2020

Yesterday, I finished my first year at UC Davis as a CS major! I wanted to write to reflect a little on how my first year went. So firstly, a major change from the start of the year to now is that I switched from the CSE to the CS major, and I now also have plans to get a minor in econ. This is something that I only decided after my winter quarter. The CS classes I took were ECS 36A, 36B, 36C, 20, and 50. I felt like i grew a lot as a CS major after this one year. I additionally worked on some cool projects on different platforms, like Komma for android, iOS, and web application. I also worked on a webpage in the #include club. I also participated in LAHacks, where me and some friends built a shopping inventory alerter in Python using the Twilio API. I grew a lot more than I thought I could as a software developor over this year. Now, I'm super excited to start my internship at T-Mobile in just about a week and a half!
